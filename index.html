How this started

It took an old hippie and a $500-a-day habit to get me to Jesus.

I was 19 and out of options. A $500-a-day habit I couldn't stop on my own. I didn't want to keep living the way I was living. Truth is, I didn't want to live at all.
And when I finally quit it, I just pivoted. I'll only drink. I'll only smoke dope. I'll only take acid and smoke dope. I'll only binge a little cocaine. Nine months of that taught me what takes some people decades: the problem was never the substance. My drug of choice was always more.
An old hippie in AA asked me one question. "You got a problem with God?" I said no. He said, "Great. Get on your knees every morning and ask God to keep you sober. At night, tell Him thank you." No church background. No religion. I was just desperate enough to follow instructions. That was 1991. I've been sober ever since.
God wasn't done — He was drawing. Years into walking with Jesus, I finally found the verse that explained my whole story. John 6:44 — no one can come to Jesus unless the Father draws him. That was me — nine years in the rooms, rejecting Him, and the Father drawing me anyway.
Then came Sidney. My unborn daughter was diagnosed with a heart defect she wouldn't survive. Losing her broke me wide open. In 2000, I gave my life to Christ — a heart choice, not a church habit. I carry Sidney's memory with honor, not shame.
And God kept restoring what life kept breaking. In 2017 a toxic brain injury scrambled the very mind I'm asking you to renew. Strokes followed in 2023. I'm still here — thirty-five years sober, mind renewed daily by the same Word that's in this app. Restoration isn't a theory to me. It's my history.
That's exactly how this app works. One honest prayer at a time. If you're where I was — broken, out of options, no church language — you're the reason this exists. The door is open. Come on in. You're welcome anytime.
